James Whitfield, CEO of Whitfield Industries, was deposed on March 18, 2024, in New York, regarding the civil action Hartwell Group v. Whitfield Industries. Whitfield provided an overview of his tenure at the company, including his role overseeing operations, his board responsibilities, and his involvement in negotiating the joint venture agreement at the center of the dispute. Whitfield described the events leading up to the alleged breach, including a series of meetings with Hartwell representatives during the fourth quarter of 2023 and the subsequent restructuring of the company's document retention policy. Regarding discovery, Whitfield stated that outside counsel conducted the search of his corporate email account and personal devices, and that he reviewed the production set after collection. He acknowledged that certain communications with board members were conducted through encrypted messaging platforms and confirmed that those messages were not retained beyond a 30-day rolling window. Whitfield discussed the company's relationship with Hartwell Group spanning the past four years, including prior contractual arrangements, the establishment of the joint venture in early 2023, and the disputed amendment dated October 12, 2023. He described his role as the primary decision-maker on the Whitfield side and noted that final approval authority on all material contract terms rested with him. Whitfield addressed questions about the company's financial transfers to affiliated entities during the relevant period, including transactions flagged in the plaintiff's exhibits. The deposition also covered Whitfield's communications practices, his approach to documenting executive decisions, and the timeline of events leading up to the filing of the complaint.
